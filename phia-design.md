# Phia-Inspired Luxury Fashion Website Style Guide

## Purpose

A condensed design document based on the public visual and editorial language of [phia.com](https://phia.com/). The goal is to capture the site’s upscale, polished, fashion-forward aesthetic without copying it literally.

## Core Style Summary

Phia presents as **editorial luxury with modern product utility**:

- high-fashion mood, but not overly ornate
- clean and premium, with selective softness and gloss
- minimalist structure paired with rich typography
- fashion-magazine storytelling layered over ecommerce/search behavior
- aspirational, feminine, curated, and quietly expensive

## Brand Feel

Use these traits as the north star:

- **Luxury, not loud**
- **Editorial, not corporate**
- **Curated, not crowded**
- **Soft sophistication, not sterile minimalism**
- **Trend-aware, not fast-fashion chaotic**

Keywords:
`posh` `elevated` `editorial` `glossy` `refined` `curated` `fashion-first` `feminine` `aspirational`

## Visual Principles

1. **Let typography carry status**  
   Serif headlines and clean sans body text do most of the luxury work.
2. **Use whitespace generously**  
   Layouts should breathe like a magazine spread.
3. **Keep the palette restrained**  
   Mostly black, white, soft gray, with occasional muted color accents.
4. **Mix polish with ease**  
   The experience should feel expensive but effortless.
5. **Favor curation over density**  
   Show fewer, stronger choices instead of overwhelming grids.

## Typography

Observed from the site CSS:

- **Display serif:** `GT Super Display`
- **Primary sans:** `PP Neue Montreal`
- **Mono utility:** `Roboto Mono`

### Recommended usage

- **Hero / section headlines:** elegant serif, light to regular weight
- **Body / UI / navigation:** clean sans serif
- **Micro labels / metadata / filters / price compare details:** mono or tightly tracked sans

### Type tone

- Headlines should feel like magazine covers
- Body copy should feel modern and understated
- Labels should feel precise, almost merchandising-like

### Suggested type scale

- Hero headline: 56–96px
- Section headline: 32–56px
- Card title: 18–24px
- Body: 16–18px
- Microcopy / labels: 12–14px

## Color Direction

Observed palette cues from the live CSS:

- Primary black: `#000000`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Soft gray background: `#F8F8F8`
- Light gray surface: `#EEEEEE`
- Tertiary text gray: `#757575`
- Accent blue present in tokens: `#084BE7`
- Secondary pale blue cue: `#97C6EA`
- Deep accent purple cue: `#582D76`

### Practical palette

**Base**

- Ink: `#000000`
- Paper: `#FFFFFF`
- Mist: `#F8F8F8`
- Line: `rgba(0,0,0,0.08)`
- Muted text: `#757575`

**Accent strategy**

- Use accent colors sparingly
- Prefer muted jewel tones or cool editorial blues
- Keep most screens neutral so product imagery and typography stand out

## Layout

### Overall structure

- Clean full-bleed sections
- Spacious margins
- Strong vertical rhythm
- Alternation between image-led moments and restrained product/content blocks

### Grid behavior

- Desktop: 12-column editorial/ecommerce hybrid grid
- Tablet: 6–8 columns
- Mobile: single-column with stacked cards and generous spacing

### Spacing feel

- Large section padding
- Air around headlines and imagery
- Limited visual clutter above the fold

## Imagery Style

Editorial page titles/descriptions suggest a strong moodboard approach:

- romantic lace
- bold luxury
- coastal European summer
- statement bags
- sultry warm hues
- vintage/antique uniqueness

### Image direction

- Use polished fashion photography, not generic catalog shots
- Prioritize tonal consistency across a page
- Blend:
  - editorial stills
  - styled product imagery
  - aspirational lifestyle references
- Cropping should feel intentional and premium
- Avoid overly saturated, noisy, or bargain-looking images

### Subject matter cues

- tailored layers
- handbags as statement objects
- refined neutrals
- occasional sensual textures: lace, leather, silk, sheer fabrics, metallic hardware
- quiet luxury silhouettes with trend-aware styling

## UI Component Style

### Navigation

- Minimal top nav
- Fine-weight sans text
- Plenty of horizontal breathing room
- Sticky header okay if visually light

### Buttons

- Clean, compact, understated
- Rounded but not bubbly
- Dark primary button on light backgrounds
- Secondary actions should feel subtle and premium

### Cards

- Editorial cards: image-first with restrained text
- Product cards: clean metadata hierarchy, minimal chrome
- Use soft borders or tonal separation instead of heavy shadows

### Inputs / filters / compare tools

- Crisp and modern
- Minimal outlines
- Small radii
- Quiet utility styling so the fashion content remains dominant

### Surfaces

The site includes glass-style surface treatments in CSS. Use lightly:

- translucent overlays
- soft blur for modals/floating surfaces
- fine borders and layered highlights
- only where it adds polish, not everywhere

## Motion

### Desired motion language

- smooth, elegant, low-friction
- fade, slide, shimmer, and soft parallax over aggressive animation
- interactions should feel expensive and calm

### Good motion choices

- hover image zoom: subtle
- text shimmer on premium callouts: acceptable in moderation
- drawer/modal transitions: soft ease, not snappy
- staggered reveal for editorial sections

### Avoid

- bouncy motion
- flashy overshoot
- loud hover effects
- anything that feels game-like or tech-startup playful

## Content Tone

From the editorial metadata, the copy voice is:

- stylish
- concise
- trend-literate
- slightly indulgent
- feminine and curated
- confident without sounding salesy

### Copy rules

- Keep lines short and sharp
- Write like a stylist, not a marketer
- Use fashion cues and mood phrases
- Avoid jargon-heavy product copy

### Example tone

- “Clean lines, minimal effort.”
- “Bags where the outfit doesn’t even matter.”
- “Bold luxury aesthetic.”
